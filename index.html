Swidnica, Poland

Cathedral

Wacław Męczennik in Świdnica - a Gothic church, originally a parish church, and from March 25, 2004 as the cathedral of the Świdnica diocese. It is located at John Paul II Square, in the south-eastern part of the medieval city and is one of the most important monuments of Świdnica. Read more.

Church of Peace

A monument from the UNESCO World Heritage List, a Monument of History, the largest wooden Baroque temple in Europe, one of the seven new wonders of Poland according to "National Geographic"... The beginnings of the Church of Peace did not herald such a string of successes. Read more.

Town Hall

For centuries, the city hall has been a symbol of the splendor and honorable traditions of Silesian cities. The first town hall tower appeared in Świdnica in the Middle Ages. Read more.

Water Tower
